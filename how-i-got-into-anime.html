How I Got Into Anime

I still remember the first time I stumbled across anime. I was bored one evening, scrolling through random videos, when I saw a clip from Naruto. I didn’t even know what anime was at the time — I just saw a kid with spiky hair screaming about becoming Hokage, and I was instantly hooked.

At first, I didn’t tell anyone I was watching it. I thought people might laugh or say it’s “just cartoons.” But the more I watched, the more I realized that anime wasn’t just about cool fights or flashy art — it was about emotions, life lessons, motivation, and heart.

Shows like One Piece taught me about loyalty and never giving up. Attack on Titan made me think deeply about freedom and sacrifice. And My Hero Academia gave me the push to keep training in real life — not to become a hero with powers, but to become the best version of myself.

Anime became more than entertainment. It became a part of my life. It helped me through boredom, loneliness, and gave me a reason to dream big.

That’s why I started this blog — to share my love for anime and connect with others who feel the same. Whether you’re just starting or you’ve seen hundreds of shows, you’re welcome here.
